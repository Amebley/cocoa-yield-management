Choosing Seeds and Growing Seedlings

Seed Selection

Growers can either:

  • Purchase selected seeds of good quality from research centers.
  • Buy young seedlings ready for planting.

If no research center is nearby, growers can still achieve a good yield by:

  1. Choosing their own seeds from healthy trees.
  2. Sowing seeds in a nursery bed.
  3. Planting seedlings in the plantation.

Nursery Bed: A place for sowing seeds to germinate.

Choosing Seeds

For high-quality cocoa trees:

  • Select large pods from prolific trees.
  • Use seeds from the middle of the pod.
  • Sow seeds immediately; avoid keeping pods for more than a week.

Sowing Seeds

In Nursery Beds

  1. Choose a flat, light, and rich soil plot.
  2. Till the soil deeply, breaking lumps.
  3. Make beds 120 cm wide, with 60 cm paths between them.
  4. Mark out furrows 25 cm apart and sow seeds 25 cm apart.
  5. Avoid planting too deep to ensure air access.

In Baskets or Bags

  • Use baskets or polyethylene bags (30 cm high, 20 cm wide).
  • Fill with fine soil mixed with manure.
  • Place baskets in rows with space between.

Care:

  • Shade seedlings to protect from sun.
  • Water daily and remove weeds.
  • Inspect for insects and disease; remove and burn affected plants.

Lifting Seedlings

  1. After 6 months, when seedlings have two leaves, gently remove from nursery beds.
  2. Sort seedlings: discard diseased or poorly grown ones.
  3. Plant seedlings:
    • If in baskets: Place baskets in holes; they will rot naturally.
    • If in bags: Remove the bag before planting.
