Harvesting the Pods

The cocoa tree typically produces its first flowers after two years. However, to avoid stressing the tree, it is advisable to remove these initial flowers and not allow them to fruit.

There are two harvests each year:

  • A small harvest at the beginning of the rainy season
  • A big harvest at the end of the rainy season

Do not pick all the pods at once. Instead, only harvest pods that are ripe, which can be either yellow or red. Leave any pods that are still green on the tree.

Visit the plantation every two weeks to pick the ripe pods. Always use a machete to cut the stem of the pod rather than pulling them off, as pulling can damage the tree.
