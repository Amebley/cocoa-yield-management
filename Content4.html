Taking Care of the Plantation

Once the cocoa trees are planted, ongoing care is essential to ensure a productive and healthy plantation. Proper maintenance includes:

  • Replacing missing seedlings
  • Weeding and covering the soil
  • Pruning the cocoa trees
  • Applying fertilizers
  • Protecting the trees from insects and diseases

Replacing Missing Seedlings

Check the plantation regularly for seedlings that are not growing well or have died. Remove and burn diseased or dead seedlings and replace them with new ones from the nursery.

Weeding and Soil Cover

Weeds compete with cocoa trees for nutrients. When trees are young, weed 4 to 5 times a year. Older trees need less weeding, approximately once a year. Avoid damaging the trunk and roots while weeding.

Cover the soil between rows with cut weeds or palm fronds to protect it from sun and erosion. This helps keep the soil moist and cool while adding organic matter as the weeds decompose. You may also sow cover crops like legumes for additional protection.

Pruning Cocoa Trees

Proper pruning helps maintain the tree’s health and productivity:

  • Remove shoots that form on the trunk and let the strongest ones develop.
  • If the crown forms too low, select a vertical shoot to form a new crown at a proper height.
  • Remove dead branches, dry twigs, and suckers close to the trunk.
  • To rejuvenate old trees, allow suckers to grow and then cut down the old trunk.

Applying Fertilizers

Use fertilizers wisely to enhance growth and yield:

  • Apply fertilizers around the base of each tree, avoiding contact with the trunk and leaves.
  • Apply twice a year, in April and September.
  • Different soils require different fertilizers. For sandy soils, use a formula of 13-10-15; for granitic soils, use 12-15-18.
  • In the first two years, apply 125 grams in April and September. After that, apply 250 grams each time.

Protection from Insects and Diseases

Protect cocoa trees from harmful insects and diseases:

Insects

  • Capsids: Use Lindane or Aldrin to control these pests that damage twigs and pods.
  • Borers: Control with DDT or Dieldrin. These larvae bore holes in the trunk or branches.

Diseases

  • Black Pod Disease: Remove and burn diseased pods, and spray healthy pods with copper preparations.
  • Swollen Shoot Disease: Cut down and burn diseased trees and all nearby trees to prevent spreading.
